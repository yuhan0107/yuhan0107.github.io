[Curriculum Vitae]


I am a PhD student at Remote Sensing of Environment and Disaster lab, Institute of Industrial Science since September 2018. My specific research interest lies in Blue carbon ecosystem assessment. My principal area of research interest is to monitor the mangrove ecosystems changes in China by remote sensing technology to study the ecological servives and blue carbon storage of mangroves. I am finishing mapping the mangrove forests for the past 20 years in China with ALOS PALSAR-2 and Landsat imagery.

I completed my PhD in Remote Sensing from The University of Tokyo (2018) under the guidance of Dr. Wataru Takeuchi where I investigated Socio-economic growth Impacts on Urban Air Quality in India. I also previously worked on developing Multi-resolution Segmentation algorithm for Polarimetric SAR classification under Dr. Onkar Dikshit for B.Tech.-M.Tech. at Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur. As a data scientist at EXL Analytics (2013-2015), I built risk forecasting models for bank credit cards. These experiences were crucial in my learnings towards remote sensing data processing and time-series modeling.
